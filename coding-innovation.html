Clinical Coding Innovation Challenge

1. Eligibility Criteria

a. Participant Status

  • Participants must be enrolled as students in a educational institution at the time of registration.
  • Both undergraduate and graduate students from various disciplines, including technology, healthcare, engineering, and business, are eligible to participate.

b. Team Composition

  • Teams may consist of 3 members.
  • Interdisciplinary teams with participants from various medical specialties or healthcare fields are encouraged.

Timeline

2. Preparation Guidelines

a. Categories

  • The competition may include categories such as General Coding, Specialty Coding, Advanced Coding

b. Focus Area

  • Participants will demonstrate their skills in coding medical records using various coding system, including ICD-10, ICD-9-CM, etc.
  • Challenges can range from diagnostics and treatment to patient engagement, wellness monitoring, or any other relevant healthcare domain.

c. Format

  • Online submission, written exams, or practical coding exercises.

d. Evaluation Process

  • Entries will be reviewed by a panel of judges including experts in medical coding and health informatics.

e. Innovation and Creativity

  • Competition can drive innovation by encouraging participants to explore and apply new coding tools, technologies, and techniques.
  • Insight gained from the contest can inform improvements in coding practices and educational programs.

f. Impact on Healthcare

  • Accurate Data: High-quality coding contributes to accurate health data, which is crucial for patient care, research, and healthcare policy making.
  • Efficient Claims Processing: Acuurate coding reduce claim rejections and denials, leading to more efficient billing and reimbursement processes.

3. Competition Structure

  • The competition may consist of three rounds, including two preliminary rounds and a final round for top-scorsing team.
  • Rounds may involve a mix of individual and team-based answering formats.

4. Submission

  • Applicants have to submit coded case studies, coding assignments, or responses to coding scenarios on or before the 30th September 2024 and only three teams will be short listed for the final round.

5. Registration

  • Teams must register for the competition by the 30th September 2024. A 100 USD registration fee per team should be paid upon registration.
  • Provide Team members names, contact information, Organization/ University, and a brief proposal of the proposed healthcare innovation challenge.

6. Judging Criteria

  • Final three submissions will be evaluated based on accuracy, compliance with coding rules and thoroughness.
  • Judges may also consider the scalability and sustainability of the proposed health care technology.

7. Prizes

  • Cash prizes include recognition, certificates, and potential exposure to industry professionals.
  • First three places will be selected from each competition and the prizes will be arranged as below:
    • 1st Place: 200 USD
    • 2nd Place: 150 USD
    • 3rd Place: 100 USD

7. Code of Conduct

  • Participants must adhere to ethical guidelines and respect intellectual property rights throughout the competition.
  • Plagiarism or unauthorized use of existing solutions will result in disqualification.
